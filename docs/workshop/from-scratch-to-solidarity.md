# From Scratch to Solidarity: Launching Your Local's Social Media

> **Public guide:** attendees and locals should use **Comms Resources** at `/[locale]/guide/resources`. This file is the facilitator run sheet for the presentation format only.

**When:** August session (date TBA)  
**Format:** Presentation + interleaved Q&A (remote lunch-and-learn friendly)  
**Audience:** Local officers and volunteer executives starting social media from scratch

## Session goal

Give a clear high-level plan for launching a local's digital presence, demo UnionOps tools live (logo, notice board, graphics), and leave attendees ready to tinker self-paced afterward. Do **not** rely on full hands-on facilitation during a remote lunch lab.

## Why this format

Remote lunch-and-learns are hard to facilitate as live labs (screen share, uneven devices, muted rooms). Run demos yourself; invite questions after each block; point everyone to the practice checklist on Comms Resources for follow-up.

**Talking point:** "You bring your lunch; we'll bring the tools. Leave with a plan — then tinker on your own time."

## Materials needed

- Facilitator screen share (or projector)
- Demo Brand Kit: `public/demo/brand-kit-local-243.json` (optional — import before session)
- Attendees: browser open to Comms Resources is enough; laptops optional for later tinkering

## Run sheet (~50–60 minutes)

### 0–12 min — Strategy essentials + Q&A

1. **Problem:** A strong digital presence informs, engages, and mobilizes — but starting from scratch overwhelms busy volunteer executives (platforms? graphics? logo?).
2. **Essentials:** Choose platforms you can sustain; secure shared accounts (not personal); set a posting rhythm that won't burn out the executive.
3. Point to **First week** (`/guide/social-media-plan`) and **Blueprint** (`/guide`) for written depth after the call.

**Discussion prompts:**
- Which platform does your local already use (or ignore) the most?
- Who holds the passwords today — and what happens if that person leaves the executive?

### 12–20 min — Identity (Logo Builder) + Q&A

Live demo: `/onboarding` → Brand Kit colours → `/tools/logo-builder` → export a circular PNG.

**Talking point:** Every strong local needs a recognizable brand. UnionOps generates a clean local logo without design software.

**Discussion prompts:**
- Do you already have official colours or letterhead from your union?
- Who should approve the logo before it goes on a Facebook cover?

### 20–28 min — Inspiration (Notice Board / Union Boards) + Q&A

Live tour: `/tools/board-notice` and/or `/guide/union-boards` — gallery of layouts for AGMs, actions, member updates, standing resources.

**Talking point:** Never stare at a blank screen — borrow real-world notice patterns, then adapt for social crops in Graphic Maker.

**Discussion prompts:**
- What one notice would your members need to see this month on a workplace board *and* online?
- Who prints and posts physical boards at your worksites?

### 28–40 min — Generating media (Graphic Maker) + Q&A

Live demo: `/tools/graphic-maker` — news graphic, quote card, or announcement; show platform-sized exports. Mention captions library if time.

**Talking point:** Skip expensive design software — sized and branded for the platforms you chose in the strategy block.

**Discussion prompts:**
- What is the next post you actually need to ship (meeting, bargaining update, member spotlight)?
- If you use member photos, who checks consent? Point to `/guide/photo-consent`.

### 40–50+ min — Close + open floor

1. Recap: strategy → identity → inspiration → media.
2. Point to **practice checklist** and **Explore the hub** on `/guide/resources`.
3. Encourage self-paced tinkering this week (no live exports required on the call).
4. Optional open Q&A.

## Attendees should tinker later

Do not require these during the call. After the session:

- [ ] Import the demo Brand Kit or complete onboarding for your local
- [ ] Export a circular local logo (PNG)
- [ ] Create and download a board notice (PDF)
- [ ] Create one social graphic with Graphic Maker
- [ ] (Optional) Download the website ZIP and skim the README
